# Executive Summary

Artificial Intelligence systems are increasingly embedded in automated credit decision processes, including underwriting, risk-based pricing, fraud detection, and limit assignment. While these systems improve efficiency and predictive performance, they introduce significant regulatory, ethical, and systemic risk.

This whitepaper presents a structured, implementation-ready AI governance architecture tailored for regulated credit environments in the United States.

The framework operationalizes:

- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)
- SR 11-7 Model Risk Management guidance
- Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
- Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
- CFPB supervisory expectations

The objective is to translate high-level regulatory principles into enforceable, auditable, and measurable governance controls.

---

# 1. The Governance Gap in AI-Driven Credit Systems

Financial institutions face a structural governance gap:

- AI models evolve faster than regulatory frameworks
- Bias risk is often tested superficially
- Model validation may not address algorithmic discrimination
- Governance documentation is frequently fragmented
- Monitoring controls lack integration across risk domains

NIST AI RMF provides principles, but institutions require implementation artifacts.

This framework bridges that gap.

# 2. Framework Architecture Overview

The AI Credit Risk Governance Framework is modular and composed of seven integrated components:

1. AI Credit Risk Taxonomy  
2. Risk Scoring Matrix  
3. Governance Audit Checklist  
4. Model Inventory Register  
5. Fair Lending Testing Framework  
6. AI Model Validation Framework  
7. Regulatory Alignment Map  

Together, these components form a complete governance lifecycle covering:

- Risk identification  
- Risk measurement  
- Independent validation  
- Fair lending controls  
- Monitoring & escalation  
- Regulatory traceability  

---

# 3. Core Governance Pillars

## 3.1 Risk Identification & Classification

Structured taxonomy enables categorization of:

- Operational risk
- Regulatory risk
- Fair lending exposure
- Explainability risk
- Systemic impact risk

The Risk Scoring Matrix quantifies risk severity, likelihood, detectability, regulatory exposure, and fairness implications.

---

## 3.2 Fair Lending & Bias Mitigation

The framework incorporates statistical bias testing using:

- Adverse Impact Ratio (AIR)
- Disparate impact analysis
- Proxy correlation testing
- Feature-level contribution analysis
- Ongoing fairness drift monitoring

This enables defensible compliance with ECOA and CFPB expectations.

---

## 3.3 Independent Model Validation

Aligned with SR 11-7, the validation framework enforces:

- Conceptual soundness review
- Data integrity verification
- Performance testing
- Stress testing
- Independent fairness verification
- Ongoing monitoring requirements

---

## 3.4 Regulatory Traceability

The Regulatory Alignment Map ensures traceable linkage between governance controls and:

- NIST AI RMF core functions
- SR 11-7 model risk principles
- ECOA non-discrimination requirements
- CFPB supervisory focus areas

This creates examination readiness.
