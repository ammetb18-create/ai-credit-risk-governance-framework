# AI Credit Risk Governance Framework

## Executive Overview

This project aims to design and implement a structured AI governance and risk mitigation framework specifically tailored to automated credit evaluation systems operating within U.S. financial institutions. As artificial intelligence and algorithmic decision-making increasingly influence credit approvals, risk scoring, and lending access, financial institutions face growing exposure to algorithmic bias, regulatory non-compliance, and systemic operational risk.

While the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) provides high-level guidance, there remains a significant implementation gap between regulatory principles and practical deployment within financial institutions. This project seeks to operationalize those standards into measurable controls, bias-detection metrics, monitoring protocols, and governance procedures applicable to real-world credit systems.

By reducing algorithmic discrimination, improving transparency, and aligning financial AI systems with national regulatory priorities, this endeavor directly supports economic stability, consumer protection, and national financial infrastructure resilience.

---

## Technical Scope

The framework will consist of the following components:

### 1. Algorithmic Risk Identification Layer
- Mapping decision variables used in automated credit scoring systems
- Identification of high-risk proxy variables
- Bias exposure mapping aligned with ECOA risk considerations

### 2. Bias Detection & Fairness Metrics Module
- Statistical disparity analysis
- Fairness ratio measurement
- Outcome distribution monitoring
- Threshold definition for acceptable variance

### 3. NIST AI RMF Operationalization Layer
- Translation of NIST AI RMF principles into measurable controls
- AI risk categorization matrix
- Governance documentation structure for audit-readiness

### 4. Continuous Monitoring & Reporting Structure
- Periodic bias auditing methodology
- Risk scoring dashboard concept
- Regulatory documentation preparation template

---

## National Importance Justification

The U.S. financial system increasingly relies on algorithmic decision-making tools to evaluate creditworthiness, manage lending risk, and optimize operational efficiency. However, the absence of standardized implementation frameworks for AI governance exposes institutions to systemic bias, regulatory enforcement actions, reputational damage, and economic instability.

This project addresses a nationally relevant problem by operationalizing federal AI governance standards within critical financial infrastructure. Given the scale of automated lending systems and their impact on consumer access to credit, employment mobility, and economic participation, strengthening algorithmic integrity directly supports national economic resilience and consumer protection priorities.

## Methodology

The implementation of this framework will follow a structured multi-phase approach:

### Phase 1 – Risk Mapping & Variable Analysis
- Identification of input variables commonly used in automated credit scoring systems
- Proxy risk evaluation
- Regulatory exposure classification

### Phase 2 – Fairness & Bias Quantification
- Statistical disparity measurement modeling
- Threshold definition for acceptable variance
- Simulation of outcome distribution shifts

### Phase 3 – Governance Control Structuring
- Mapping NIST AI RMF principles into operational control checkpoints
- Creation of documentation templates for institutional audit readiness
- Definition of accountability roles and review cycles

### Phase 4 – Monitoring & Continuous Improvement
- Development of periodic bias audit protocols
- Risk scoring update logic
- Governance reporting structure
